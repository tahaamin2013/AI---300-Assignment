# Summary Writing Best Practices

## Principles of Effective Summaries

### 1. Clarity and Conciseness
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid jargon unless necessary for the audience
- Prefer active voice over passive voice
- Eliminate redundant words and phrases

### 2. Accuracy and Fidelity
- Preserve the original meaning and intent
- Don't add information not present in the source
- Don't omit critical information
- Maintain logical connections between ideas

### 3. Completeness
- Include all essential information
- Cover the main points, not just interesting details
- Maintain the structure's logical flow
- Ensure the summary can stand alone

### 4. Relevance
- Focus on information most important to the reader
- Consider the summary's purpose and audience
- Prioritize actionable information
- Emphasize implications and consequences

## Structure Guidelines

### Introduction (1-2 sentences)
- State the main topic or purpose
- Provide necessary context
- Set the scope for what follows

### Body (3-5 key points)
- Present information in logical order
- Use clear transitions between points
- Maintain proportion (important points get more space)
- Support key points with essential details only

### Conclusion (1-2 sentences)
- Summarize the main takeaway
- Highlight implications or next steps
- Provide closure to the summary

## Language and Style

### Word Choice
- Use precise, specific words
- Avoid vague terms like "things," "stuff," "aspects"
- Replace complex words with simpler alternatives when possible
- Use concrete examples instead of abstract concepts when appropriate

### Sentence Structure
- Keep sentences short and direct (15-25 words)
- Use simple sentence structures
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Avoid complex subordinate clauses

### Tone and Voice
- Match the formality level of the source material
- Use consistent tense throughout
- Maintain objectivity and neutrality
- Avoid personal opinions or interpretations

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. Information Loss
- Don't oversimplify complex concepts
- Don't remove essential context
- Don't eliminate necessary qualifications
- Don't ignore counter-arguments or limitations

### 2. Information Addition
- Don't insert personal opinions
- Don't add examples not in the original
- Don't interpret ambiguous information
- Don't fill gaps with assumptions

### 3. Structural Issues
- Don't change the original logic flow
- Don't combine unrelated points
- Don't omit necessary transitions
- Don't ignore the source's organization

### 4. Language Problems
- Don't use overly complex vocabulary
- Don't employ vague or ambiguous terms
- Don't create run-on sentences
- Don't use inconsistent terminology

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a summary, check:

□ **Length**: Is it appropriately concise (10-20% of original)?
□ **Accuracy**: Does it preserve the original meaning?
□ **Completeness**: Are all essential points included?
□ **Clarity**: Can it be understood without the original text?
□ **Relevance**: Is the information important to the intended audience?
□ **Structure**: Does it have clear introduction, body, and conclusion?
□ **Language**: Is the writing clear and direct?
□ **Objectivity**: Is it free from personal bias or interpretation?
